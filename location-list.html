7 Businesses in Downtown Columbia

Downtown Columbia, Mo., is known for its wide range of businesses catering to all kinds of people. These are, in my opinion, some of the best ones that I’ve found during my time as a student of Mizzou, located right beside the downtown area.

Poppy, Made by Hand

920 E Broadway #1,
Columbia, MO 65201
(573) 442-3223
Visit their website here

Poppy, Made by Hand is an excellent shop if you’re looking for a gift for a loved one or yourself! There’s a wide variety of products and it’s on the more expensive side, but it’s all high-quality items. From posters and stationery to amazing handmade jewelry, even candles, soaps, and other bath products. Nearly all of their products, especially ceramics and pottery, are acquired from local artists.

Peace Nook

804 E. Broadway C,
Columbia, MO 65201
(573) 875-0539
Visit their website here

This local business is a non-profit volunteer-based shop under the Peaceworks organization in Columbia. The products sold are almost all handmade by local artists and are centered around sustainability. It definitely has that hippie vibe, but it’s a great atmosphere. You will feel very welcome every time you go here.

Iron Moe's

816 E. Broadway,
Columbia, MO 65201
(573) 489-2643
Visit their website here

Iron Moe’s is a relatively new tattoo shop above Broadway Brewery. Opened by Moe himself, there are a total of two licensed artists and three apprentices under them. They specialize in fine-line tattoos and have a very simplistic design feel to all of their projects. I got my first tattoo here and I’m trying to go back from another. The atmosphere is great and I recommend it for anyone getting their first tattoo as well.

Goldie's Bagels

114 S. 9th Str.
Suite 102
Columbia, MO 65201
Contact them here
Visit their website here

Goldie’s Bagels is an infamous new business on 9th Street. Started by Amanda Rainey, Goldie’s Bagels is a bagel bakery specializing in Jewish traditional recipes. It’s been a very popular place for townies and they have been doing their best to give back to their community.

Shortwave Coffee

915 Alley A,
Columbia, MO 65201
(573) 214-0880
Visit their website here

While I haven’t been to this location much, it’s a great place to go with friends. I favor the 9th Street location as it’s easy to pop in an out, but the atmosphere that the Alley A location provides is very unique. I love Shortwave coffee. They always have such high quality espresso and have unique drink recipes. As a barista myself, I appreciate a good espresso pull with a different recipe. The Alley A location is located in this area that makes it feel like an industrial basement. There aren’t any windows which provides a moodier setting and they play soft music to pair it with.

Hitt Records

10 Hitt Str.,
Columbia, MO 65201
(573) 777-9299
Visit their website here

Hitt Records is located in the same building as Uprise Bakery and Ragtag Cinema. They even have a connecting door leading to each other. On a weekend, when you’re craving some good food, you can pop into Uprise for a lunch then venture over to Hitt Records to browse their collection of new and vintage vinyl records as well as CDs by various artists.

Fretboard Coffee

1013 E. Walnut Str.,
Columbia, MO 65201
(573) 227-2233
Visit their website here

Fretboard Coffee is a smaller coffee shop located in the Norht Arts District. It is surrounded by great community events. Every month, on the first Friday, the Arts District holds First Friday where various art galleries are free entry and businesses offer discounts. Fretboard hosts different musical artists for this event every First Friday.
